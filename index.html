Hi, I’m Vivian (Yiwei) Chen, a first year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of California, San Diego. I am fortunate enough to be jointly advised by Prof. Taylor Berg-Kirkpatrick and Prof. Rose Yu.

Research

My research centers on LLM agents and AI for science. I’m particularly interested in agentic evaluation — understanding how agents acquire evidence, reason causally, and commit to decisions under uncertainty. I’m also interested in reinforcement learning for agents, causal and statistical reasoning, data-efficient modeling, and low-resource NLP.

My current work at UCSD includes ACED-Bench, a benchmark for evaluating whether LLM agents can actively acquire causal evidence and act on it, and Zephyrus, an agentic framework for weather science — both advised by Prof. Taylor Berg-Kirkpatrick and Prof. Rose Yu. Previously, I worked with Prof. Kilian Weinberger on diffusion-guided language modeling (Findings, ACL 2024) and with Prof. Yoav Artzi on retrospective learning from interactions (ACL 2025 Oral).
